Colours

The colours we choose on our site reflect the brand image to users.
The shades change depending on whether we are using a dark or light theme, but the hues should remain familial.

Theme Colours

Theme colours should display elements with sufficient contrast. WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) describe a ratio of 4.5:1 for saturation of text against dark surfaces. This means we should use unsaturated colours in dark themes, and saturated colours for light themes.

Secondary Colours

The size of a line should be 40-60% of the screen width for optimal readability. Any more would make guiding the reader's eye to a new line confusing. Any less might make the text seem long and difficult to track.

Each line should contain between 45-85 characters - font size should be scaled to maintain this ratio.

Spacing

Tracking (Letter-spacing): The horizontal white space between letters in a word. The ideal tracking for most text is approximately 12% (0.12).

Line spacing: The vertical space between two lines of text. The ideal space is approximately 130-150% of the height of the line.
