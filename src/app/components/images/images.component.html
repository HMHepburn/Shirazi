Images

Images convey brand style. The choice of filters, brightness, saturation, and contrast can impact how the viewer percieves Radish.

There are also different types of imagery. Graphics, icons, profile-pictures, 3D renders, and regular photos.

This article will mainly focus on graphics/restaurant logos, and photos.

Style/Demo

Photos

It is important to get appropriate images from the client when displaying products.

The optimal product image is high resolution with a good showcase of the product. Ideally multiple images should be displayed for a single product.

Photos on the same page/component should be the same size and style.

(Photos should also have rounded edges)

Graphics

Graphics are used to catch the viewer's eye and draw them in.

Each restaurant brand has its own logo. These should be presented to echo the restaurant's brand.

Graphic style should be consistent within a brand page.

Ex. Thai Express brand design:
The logo colours are present throughout the design of the thai express homepage.

The logo is high-resolution and centered.

Size

Ideally, photos should be high resolution so that they can be scaled up or down easily. (500kB-1MB for a large image, smaller images can be 300kB or less)

PNGs are ideal for logos/graphics because they allow for transparent backgrounds, while JPEGs are best for regular photos.

Typography

Alternative text is important for accessibility, as well as providing info if an image source is missing.

In addition, captions are also useful. Images should be placed beside applicable text.

Adding captions/alt text can improve the SEO ranking of a site. Naming image files relevantly will also improve SEO scores (i.e. hamburger-with-fries.jpeg vs 48dsj459dh.jpeg)
